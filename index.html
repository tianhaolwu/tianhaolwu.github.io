Working Papers

  • Monetary Reform and Energy Market Integration in Shanghai’s Coal Market, 1933–1937 []

    This study examines the impact of the 1935 Chinese Currency Reform on coal market integraQon in Shanghai by using the 1935 and 1937 publications of the “Annual Report of Shanghai Commodity Prices” to shed light on the impacts of the broader monetary reform on the energy market and the economy. The study found that the 1935 monetary reform had no significant short-term effect on market integration but concluded that it successfully reduced price volatility in both domestic and imported coal, with imported coal experiencing greater stability gains. Overall, the study reveals that promoting market integration through exchange rate reforms solely is insufficient despite increased price stability, and policymakers should incorporate such monetary reforms with other regulatory measures to achieve the desired outcome.
