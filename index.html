Curriculum Vitae

I am currently a third-year visiting undergraduate student at Harvard University and a BSc Economic History and Geography candidate at the London School of Economics (LSE). At LSE, I was the first student to be awarded with both the Lilian Knowles Prize for the Best Set of First-Year Marks and the Baines Prize for the Best Mark in EH101 . I am also the Co-Editor-in-Chief for the Knowles Review of Economic History, a student-led journal published by the LSE Houghton Street Press. At Harvard, I am a project lead at the Charles River Economic Labs and am affiliated with The Institute for Quantitative Social Science. In the summer of 2025, I worked as a policy intern at the State Administration for Market Regulation of China, and currently I am an intern at the United Nations ESCAP's Macroeconomic and Financing for Development Division.

I aspire to conduct research that translates into real-world policy influence. I am broadly interested in development policy, political economy, energy policy, and international economics, with a regional focus on China and East Asia.

You can contact me by email at twu@college.harvard.edu or t.wu36@lse.ac.uk.
